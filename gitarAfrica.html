Tishoumaren

What is Tishoumaren?

Tishoumaren, also known as desert blues, is a style of music based in The Sahara/Sahel region of northern and West Africa. Countries that are usually included in this genre is:

  • Mali
  • Niger
  • Libya
  • Western Sahara
  • Burkino Faso

Guitar has quickly become a traditional instrument in this area, despite it's origins. An instrument that is also traditionally played in West Africa is the Kora, a twenty-one stringed instrument that is similar to a harp. The genre was created first by the Tuareg or Tamasheq people. Young men who often wandered around town would sing sons with thei guitar, and they were called Ishumar. The originators of this genre wa a group of Ishumar who formed their group in camps, called Tinariwen (More of them later). The music style of Tishoumaren mixes electric blues with Middle Eastern and African sounds. The music is usually played with a tende drum. My Favorite Tishoumaren Musicians

Ali Farka Toure

Ali Ibrahim "Ali Farka" Toure was a Malian singer who popularized African Guitar music outside of Africa. He was ranked 76 on the Rolling Stone's list of the 100 Greatest Guitarists of all time, and 37 on Spin magazine's "100" Greatest Guitarists of all Time. He had 10 siblings, but was the only one to live past infancy. In Africa, it is customary to give a child a strange nickname if you've had other children who died. His was "Farka", meanig donkey. He is an extremely good guitarists and he probably has some of my favorite songs ever.

Les Filles de Illighadad

Les Filles de Illighadad is a girl group founded by Fatou Seidi Ghali. She is known as the first Tuareg woman to play guitar professionally! Their age is officially unknown, as they have no birth certificates, but they say they are in their 20s. They sing about life as nomads, religion, and folk songs passed down through generations.
